About Me

Applied Scientist & ML Engineer

I design and train vision model architectures for edge deployment — building efficient models from the ground up on large-scale GPU clusters and optimizing them to run on NPUs across diverse hardware vendors. My day-to-day lives at the intersection of architecture design, distributed training, and the constraints of real silicon.

Before industry, my research focused on LiDAR-based forest ecology — mapping biomass, carbon stocks, and biodiversity to model wildfire impacts on forest ecosystems. I hold a PhD from the University of Nevada, Reno, with publications spanning IEEE ICIP, ACM PEARC, ISVC, Machine Vision & Applications, and AGU.

Current Focus

Efficient Vision at the Edge

I architect vision models purpose-built for edge NPUs — designing and training on large-scale GPU clusters, then compressing to run at real-time latency across a fragmented hardware landscape.

Model Compression

Quantization-aware training, post-training quantization, and low-rank adaptation to shrink large vision models without sacrificing the accuracy that matters in production.

Efficient Architectures

Rethinking attention for deployment — windowed and sparse mechanisms, knowledge distillation, and architecture search tuned to the memory and latency budgets of real hardware.

World Models

Exploring joint-embedding predictive architectures that learn internal representations of the visual world — building systems that predict and reason, not just classify.
