RESEARCH INTERESTS

Overview

My primary research interests are in LiDAR pointcloud classification and part segmentation using Deep Learning.
My focal area is in the field of forest ecology, where I am interested in the use of LiDAR pointclouds to map forest biomass, carbon stocks, and biodiversity to help model and predict the effects of wildfires and climate change on forest ecosystems.

Currently, I am working on my Masters Thesis at the University of Nevada, Reno, where I am analyzing methods for LiDAR pointcloud part-segmentation on trees using a combination of Deep Learning and traditional Machine Learning techniques.

To help with this research, I am developing open-source tools to help with the processing and analysis of synthetic LiDAR pointclouds and their corresponding ground-truth labels produced by the SpeedTree modeling software.
The goal of this research is to help improve the accuracy, efficiency, and scalability of LiDAR pointcloud estimates for topological, geometrical and volumetric details of the woody structure of the tree.
